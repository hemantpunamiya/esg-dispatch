📋 Regulatory & Policy "The rulebook just got a lot thicker."
🔥 Breaking
SEBI Constitutes Working Group to Overhaul ESG Ratings Framework
India's market regulator SEBI has formed a multi-stakeholder working group — including issuers, investors, ESG rating providers, and academics — to comprehensively review and improve the existing ESG Ratings Provider regulations. The review focuses on boosting transparency, reliability, and investor confidence in ESG scores that increasingly shape capital flows across the country.
Source: ESG Today
📌 Watch
Parliamentary Committee Calls for Dedicated ESG Oversight Body and Stronger Directors' Duties
India's Parliamentary Standing Committee on Finance has recommended creating a dedicated ESG oversight body under the Ministry of Corporate Affairs and amending the Companies Act to enshrine ESG objectives as part of directors' fiduciary duties. The committee also called for mandatory independent ESG committees on company boards — modelled after audit committees — signalling a major legislative upgrade on the horizon.
Source: Ropes & Gray / Parliamentary Report
🏢 Large Company Actions "Big moves. Bigger stakes."
🏆 Top Performer
Lupin Scores 91/100 in S&P ESG Assessment, Tops India and Global Pharma
Indian pharmaceutical giant Lupin Limited has been ranked the world's top pharmaceutical company in the S&P Global Corporate Sustainability Assessment (CSA), achieving a near-perfect score of 91 out of 100 — and placing first among all sectors in India. Included in the Sustainability Yearbook 2026 for the third consecutive year, Lupin's result sets a new benchmark for ESG performance in Indian industry.
Source: Business Standard / S&P Global CSA
🟢 Milestone
IIFL Finance Posts 46-Point Score in Debut S&P Global Sustainability Assessment
Leading NBFC IIFL Finance has scored 46 in its very first participation in the S&P Global Corporate Sustainability Assessment — a sharp jump from its earlier internal baseline of 31 — reflecting a shift from disclosure-led compliance to genuinely embedded ESG performance across operations. The company's debut places it ahead of many Indian financial peers and signals rising investor confidence.
Source: Business Standard
♻️ Carbon Capture & Decarbonisation "Lab coats working overtime."
💰 Budget Signal
India's Union Budget 2026-27 Earmarks ₹20,000 Crore for CCUS Across Heavy Industries
In a landmark first, India's Union Budget 2026-27 ring-fences approximately ₹20,000 crore (~$2.2 billion) over five years specifically for Carbon Capture, Utilisation and Storage (CCUS) across five hard-to-abate sectors: power, steel, cement, refineries, and chemicals. With ₹500 crore allocated in the first year and CBAM pressure rising, this signals India's intent to decarbonise industry without sacrificing development.
Source: Down to Earth / Carbon Brief
🔬 Milestone
NTPC and IIT Bombay Complete India's First CO₂ Storage Test Well in Jharkhand
NTPC Limited, in collaboration with IIT Bombay, has completed the country's first CO₂ geological storage test well in Jharkhand — with a second monitoring well currently underway. While modest in scope, the milestone is foundational for India's CCUS ambitions: underground geological storage is the backbone of any credible carbon capture ecosystem, and this test lays the groundwork for future commercial-scale projects.
Source: Business Standard / ORF America

📋 Regulatory & Policy "Governments doing government things — but greener."
⏸️ Watch
EU's CSRD "Stop-the-Clock" Delay Reshapes Global Sustainability Reporting Calendar
The European Union has approved a two-year delay to the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD), pushing first mandatory reporting to FY 2027 for EU companies. Alongside a scope reduction of approximately 90% and the removal of listed SMEs from the mandate, the "Stop-the-Clock" measure aims to reduce regulatory burden — but the EU's Environmental Crime Directive still requires national transposition by 21 May 2026, keeping pressure on corporates.
Source: QIMA / Hogan Lovells
🐉 Policy Move
China's Unified Green Finance Catalogue Forces Banks and Corporates to Reassess Green Labels
China's People's Bank of China, the National Financial Regulatory Administration, and the CSRC jointly issued the Green Finance Endorsed Project Catalogue (2025 Edition) — now the single national taxonomy for all green financial products. The tighter definitions are forcing financial institutions to reassess existing green-labelled assets, expanding eligible categories to include industrial decarbonisation and recycling while reducing greenwashing risk across the world's second-largest economy.
Source: Hogan Lovells
🏢 Large Company Actions "When boardrooms feel the heat — literally."
📅 Deadline
Chinese Listed Companies Face 30 April Deadline for First ISSB-Aligned Sustainability Reports
Shanghai, Shenzhen, and Beijing stock exchanges have begun enforcing their mandatory sustainability reporting guidelines, with a hard 30 April 2026 deadline for companies to publish their 2025 sustainability reports aligned with ISSB standards. This marks the first mandatory ESG reporting wave in China, bringing hundreds of major companies into scope simultaneously and signalling that Asian capital markets are rapidly aligning with global disclosure norms.
Source: Latham & Watkins
⚖️ Legal
French Courts Rule Corporate Carbon Neutrality Claims Can Constitute Greenwashing
In a significant precedent for global corporate sustainability communications, French courts have ruled that public statements about carbon neutrality and energy transition can amount to greenwashing when they misrepresent a company's actual trajectory or investment strategy. The ruling is sending shockwaves through European boardrooms, where marketing-driven sustainability claims are now firmly in the legal crosshairs — particularly as ESG litigation escalates across multiple jurisdictions.
Source: Morrison Foerster
♻️ Carbon Capture & Decarbonisation "Sucking carbon from the air like it owes us money."
🌊 Live
Northern Lights (Norway) Scales to 800,000 Tonnes CO₂ Per Year — World's First Open-Access Storage Hub
Norway's Northern Lights project — the world's first open-source CO₂ transport and storage infrastructure — is now operating at capacity to capture, liquefy, and permanently store up to 800,000 tonnes of CO₂ annually beneath the North Sea seabed. Over its 15-year lifespan, the project will sequester up to 12 million tonnes of CO₂, while €5 billion in EU funding continues to push carbon capture from scattered pilots toward coordinated industrial-scale deployment.
Source: Carbon Credits / Science Times
🔬 Breakthrough
Cool Planet Technologies Validates Third-Generation Membrane Carbon Capture Module
Cool Planet Technologies has announced the successful testing of its third-generation carbon capture membrane module, marking what the company describes as a transformative milestone in the industrial scaling of its technology. The advance is significant for hard-to-abate industries like cement, where conventional CCS approaches remain costly — membrane-based capture promises a lighter-footprint, more scalable alternative for industrial decarbonisation.
Source: World Cement

Selected organisations will receive financial backing, engineering support, technical mentorship, and access to Google's infrastructure to scale their solutions — underscoring the growing convergence of AI capability and ESG ambition at the world's largest tech companies.

A clear shift is underway in European and global ESG software: platforms are moving away from simple checklists toward governed AI — systems that apply artificial intelligence with clear rules, permissions, audit trails, and human oversight, ensuring that ESG data used in official regulatory reports is traceable and defensible. With 81% of executives already using AI for sustainability goals (Deloitte, 2025), the focus is now on quality and accountability rather than just adoption.
