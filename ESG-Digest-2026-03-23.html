Carbon Market
India Launches Carbon Market Portal at Prakriti 2026 Conference
Union Power Minister Manohar Lal inaugurated the Prakriti 2026 International Conference on Carbon Markets on 21 March and launched the Indian Carbon Market Portal (indiancarbonmarket.gov.in). The Carbon Credit Trading Scheme now has 9 approved methodologies, over 40 registered entities submitting projects in biogas, hydrogen and forestry, and nearly 490 obligated entities across seven energy-intensive industries with notified targets.
Source: Press Information Bureau (21 March 2026)
Context: This is the single most important infrastructure milestone for India's compliance carbon market. The portal centralises administration of the CCTS — from project registration to credit issuance. With 490 obligated entities and targets already notified, this moves India from "carbon market framework" to "carbon market operations." The next test: whether credit pricing and liquidity follow.
Budget
A Bit of a Blur Over India's ₹20,000 Crore Carbon Credit Plan
The Union Budget 2026's ₹20,000 crore allocation for a "carbon credit programme" has sparked confusion — is it for industrial CCUS or agricultural carbon farming? The Hindu's analysis confirms the focus is on Carbon Capture, Utilisation & Storage for hard-to-abate sectors (power, steel, cement, refineries), not farmer income. Agriculture is excluded from CCUS due to its diffuse, biologically mediated emissions.
Source: The Hindu (18 March 2026)
Context: This ambiguity matters because it sets unrealistic expectations for farmers while obscuring the real industrial decarbonisation intent. The DST's "R&D Roadmap for CCUS" explicitly targets concentrated emissions from heavy industry. Stakeholder clarity is essential — otherwise the ₹20,000 crore risks becoming a political football rather than a decarbonisation tool.
Large Company Actions "A chairman quits. Crypto founders arrested. BlackRock asks pointed questions. India's governance G-factor just got real."
Governance
HDFC Bank Chairman Resigns Citing Ethical Concerns — ₹1 Lakh Crore Market Cap Wiped
Atanu Chakraborty resigned as HDFC Bank's part-time chairman on 18 March, stating "certain happenings and practices within the bank over the last two years are not in congruence with my personal values and ethics." Shares crashed 8.7% intraday, ADRs fell 7.5%, and ~₹1 lakh crore ($12B) in market cap was erased. BlackRock's Prashant Periwal told the emergency investor call: "So far, whatever I heard doesn't make me any wiser than I was an hour ago."
Source: CNBC / Bloomberg / Business Standard (18–20 March 2026)
Context: This is India's biggest governance ESG event since the IL&FS collapse. The RBI backed the bank ("no material concerns"), and Keki Mistry was appointed interim chairman for three months. But the real signal is BlackRock's public frustration — with FIIs holding ~48% of HDFC Bank, the "G" in ESG just moved from abstract disclosure to concrete capital risk. InGovern's Shriram Subramanian called on the bank to "come clean on the issues raised."
Investment
KKR to Invest Up to $310 Million in India Electric Bus Platform Allfleet
Global private equity firm KKR announced a strategic investment of up to $310 million in Allfleet, an Indian electric bus platform. The deal signals growing institutional appetite for India's transport electrification — a sector critical to urban emissions reduction and aligned with India's National Electric Mobility Mission.
Source: ESG Today (19 March 2026)
Context: India aims to electrify 80% of city bus fleets by 2030. KKR's investment follows Convergence Energy's $800M EV leasing play and validates that transport decarbonisation in India is now bankable at PE scale. For ESG investors, this is the "E" translating directly into deal flow.
Governance
CoinDCX Co-Founders Arrested in ₹71 Lakh Fraud Probe — Company Blames 1,212 Fake Websites
CoinDCX co-founders Sumit Gupta and Neeraj Khandelwal were arrested by Thane Police on 22 March following an FIR alleging a ₹71.6 lakh ($85,000) crypto fraud. CoinDCX called the FIR "false and filed as a conspiracy by impersonators posing as Founders." The company disclosed that between April 2024 and January 2026, over 1,212 fake websites impersonating coindcx.com were identified — including coindcx.pro, which was used to defraud the complainant with promises of high returns and franchise rights.
Source: Business Today / The Block / Economic Times (21–22 March 2026)
Context: This case sits at the intersection of fintech governance and digital impersonation risk. Whether the founders are culpable or victims, the episode exposes a systemic governance gap: when 1,212 fake domains can operate for 21 months before a fraud complaint triggers action, investor protection infrastructure is failing. For India's crypto ecosystem — already navigating a 30% TDS regime and uncertain regulation — this is a governance credibility test. Coinbase is a backer of CoinDCX, making this a global investor concern too.
Carbon & Decarbonisation "India's grid is about to double. The question is whether the green part doubles faster than the grey."
Energy
India's Installed Power Capacity to Double to 1,121 GW by 2036 — 70% Non-Fossil
The Central Electricity Authority's 20th Electric Power Survey projects India's installed generation capacity will more than double from 520 GW to 1,121 GW by 2035-36. The mix: 509 GW solar, 155 GW wind, 78 GW large hydro, 22 GW nuclear, 315 GW coal, and 888 GWh of storage. Non-fossil sources will contribute roughly 70% of total capacity.
Source: Bloomberg / Business Standard (19 March 2026)
Context: Solar alone will go from ~90 GW today to 509 GW — a nearly 6x increase. But 315 GW of coal capacity remaining in 2036 means India's transition is an "and" story, not an "or" story. The 888 GWh storage target is the critical enabler — without it, renewable intermittency makes the 70% non-fossil share aspirational rather than operational. Peak demand is projected at 459 GW by decade-end.

Regulatory & Policy "The world's biggest investor just told 7,200 companies: manage nature risk or face divestment."
Biodiversity
Norway's $2.1 Trillion Wealth Fund Sets Comprehensive Nature Risk Expectations
Norges Bank Investment Management (NBIM) published its first consolidated Nature Expectations for portfolio companies — merging water, ocean sustainability and biodiversity requirements into a single framework. Companies that "depend on or significantly influence ecosystems shall integrate these considerations in governance, strategy, risk management, measurement and reporting." NBIM holds stakes in ~7,200 companies; non-responsive firms face risk-based divestment assessment.
Source: ESG Today / NBIM (19 March 2026)
Context: When the world's largest sovereign wealth fund sets expectations, it's not a suggestion — it's a capital allocation signal. NBIM's eight core expectations require board-level oversight, TNFD/ISSB-aligned reporting, and time-bound targets. Portfolio companies currently score an average of just 36/100 on nature. For Indian exporters in mining, agriculture and forestry with European investor exposure, this is a compliance wake-up call.
US Climate
24 US States Sue Trump Administration Over Repeal of Endangerment Finding
A coalition of 24 US states and 15 cities filed legal action against the Trump administration's repeal of the Clean Air Act's endangerment finding — the legal basis for federal greenhouse gas regulation since 2009. The lawsuit argues the repeal undermines foundational climate protections and violates established scientific evidence.
Source: ESG Today (20 March 2026)
Context: The endangerment finding is the legal bedrock of US climate regulation. Its repeal doesn't just affect the US — it signals to global markets that federal-level climate policy is in retreat. But 24 states suing means the regulatory floor is now being fought state-by-state. For multinationals, this makes US climate compliance a patchwork — California, New York, and others will likely tighten while federal rules loosen.
Large Company Actions "Standard Chartered ties $435M to sustainability. ING's green finance jumps 28%. The money is following the mandate."
Sustainable Finance
Standard Chartered & COFCO Sign $435M Sustainability-Linked Loan
Standard Chartered and Chinese agribusiness giant COFCO signed a $435 million loan with supply chain sustainability targets tied to the pricing. The deal links financing costs to measurable ESG performance across COFCO's agricultural supply chain — one of the largest sustainability-linked commodity finance deals this year.
Source: ESG Today (19 March 2026)
Context: Sustainability-linked loans are moving from "nice to have" to the dominant structure for commodity finance. When the world's largest grain trader ties $435M to supply chain ESG, it creates a ripple effect — suppliers must demonstrate sustainability performance to access competitive capital. For Indian agri-exporters in COFCO's supply chain, this directly impacts financing terms.
Banking
ING's Sustainable Finance Activity Jumps 28% in 2025
Dutch banking group ING reported a 28% increase in sustainable finance activity in 2025, reflecting accelerating demand for green and sustainability-linked products across its corporate and institutional client base. The growth outpaced overall lending expansion, signalling that sustainable finance is gaining market share rather than just volume.
Source: ESG Today (19 March 2026)
Context: 28% growth in a single year isn't incremental — it's structural shift. European banks are now competing on green product capabilities. For Indian corporates raising international debt, the message is clear: sustainability-linked instruments are no longer the premium option — they're becoming the default for better pricing.
Carbon Pricing
EU's von der Leyen Pledges to Revise Carbon Pricing System, Introduce €30B Cleantech Fund
European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen announced commitments to revise the EU's carbon pricing system and launch a €30 billion Cleantech Fund. The revision aims to address competitiveness concerns while maintaining climate ambition — a balancing act as the EU faces industrial relocation pressure and geopolitical energy disruption.
Source: ESG Today (20 March 2026)
Context: The €30B Cleantech Fund is the EU's answer to America's IRA. Combined with CBAM revisions, this signals that the EU is recalibrating its climate industrial policy — not retreating from it. Indian exporters to the EU, particularly in steel, aluminium and cement, should watch closely: CBAM is being refined, not relaxed.

Energy Crisis
IEA Declares Biggest Oil Supply Disruption in History — Releases Record 400M Barrels
The Iran conflict has shut down the Strait of Hormuz, erasing 20 million barrels per day of petroleum flow and triggering what the IEA calls "the biggest supply disruption in the history of the oil market." Brent crude surged from $70 to nearly $120 before settling around $100. The IEA coordinated a 400-million-barrel release across 32 member nations — the largest strategic reserves deployment ever — and urged consumers to work from home, reduce highway speeds, and cut air travel.
Source: CNBC / IEA / The Guardian (20 March 2026)
Context: This is an ESG inflection point disguised as a geopolitical crisis. Every barrel of disrupted supply strengthens the investment case for energy diversification, renewables, and energy security. For India — importing ~85% of its crude — the crisis validates the accelerated renewable buildout and green hydrogen push. Oil at $100 makes solar economics even more compelling, and the CEA's 1,121 GW projection now looks like risk management, not ambition.
Conflict & Climate
The Environmental Cost of the Iran War: Oil Spills, Acid Rain and a Water Crisis
The Iran conflict is inflicting catastrophic environmental damage alongside its human toll. The war has already emitted an estimated 5 million tonnes of CO₂ — equivalent to the combined annual carbon footprint of 84 small countries. 85 large oil tankers are trapped in the Persian Gulf, creating what Greenpeace calls an unprecedented oil spill risk threatening coral reefs, mangroves and seagrass meadows. Strikes on desalination plants — including one on Qeshm Island — have cut water supply to 30 villages. Heavy smoke from burning oil infrastructure is causing hazardous air pollution across Iran. UNEP has identified over 300 incidents with potential environmental impacts since the conflict escalated.
Source: The Guardian / Greenpeace / UNEP (21 March 2026)
Context: 5 million tonnes of CO₂ — the combined footprint of 84 countries — in weeks. That's the "E" in ESG at wartime scale. Iran was already approaching "water bankruptcy" after five consecutive years of drought. The conflict's environmental costs — from desalination destruction to trapped tanker spill risk — will outlast any ceasefire. For ESG frameworks, this raises an uncomfortable question: how do portfolio companies with Gulf supply chain exposure account for conflict-driven environmental risk? For comparison, Russia-Ukraine emitted 311M tonnes CO₂e over three years; the Iran conflict is accumulating its climate debt at a far faster rate.
Geothermal
Fervo Secures $421M to Build US Geothermal Energy Plant
Geothermal developer Fervo Energy secured $421 million in funding to build a commercial-scale enhanced geothermal system in the United States. The project represents a breakthrough for next-generation geothermal technology — a 24/7 renewable energy source that doesn't depend on sun or wind.
Source: ESG Today (19 March 2026)
Context: Enhanced geothermal is the "baseload renewable" that grid operators have been waiting for. Unlike solar and wind, it produces power around the clock without storage. At $421M, this is moving from lab to grid. India has significant geothermal potential in the Himalayas, Puga Valley and Cambay basin — but regulatory and exploration frameworks remain underdeveloped.
Climate Intel
Earth Blox Raises $8M for Climate & Nature Risk Assessment
Earth Blox, a satellite-based environmental intelligence company, raised $8 million to expand its climate and nature risk assessment platform. The tool uses earth observation data to help companies and investors quantify physical climate risks and nature-related exposures across portfolios and supply chains.
Source: ESG Today (19 March 2026)
Context: This sits at the intersection of two megatrends: TNFD-aligned nature reporting (see NBIM's expectations above) and satellite-based MRV. As corporate nature risk disclosure moves from voluntary to expected, platforms like Earth Blox become critical data infrastructure. Last week's AIRMO raise for space-based GHG detection and this week's Earth Blox round confirm: climate intelligence is now a fundable asset class.
