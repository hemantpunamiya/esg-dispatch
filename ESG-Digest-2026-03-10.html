SEBI Convenes Multi-Stakeholder Working Group to Overhaul ESG Ratings Oversight
India's securities regulator has launched a sweeping review of the framework governing ESG Rating Providers, assembling a working group of issuers, investors, domestic and global ratings firms, legal experts and academics. The group is tasked with recommending measures to enhance transparency, improve methodology reliability, and align India's ESG ratings ecosystem with global best practices — as capital flows into sustainable finance accelerate.
Source: ESG News / SEBI
👀 Watch
SEBI's BRSR Value Chain Reporting Goes Live for Top 250 Listed Companies This FY
India's top 250 listed entities must now voluntarily assess and disclose ESG data across their supply chains — covering upstream and downstream partners accounting for 2% or more of purchases or sales. The phased rollout of BRSR Core supply chain reporting is reshaping accountability expectations, pushing ESG beyond the corporate boundary and into every tier of the value chain.
Source: Mongabay India / SEBI BRSR Framework

🔥 Breaking
India's Budget 2026-27 Allocates ₹20,000 Crore ($2.2B) for CCUS in Hard-to-Abate Sectors
In a historic first, India's Union Budget 2026-27 ringfences ₹20,000 crore specifically for Carbon Capture, Utilisation and Storage (CCUS) targeting power, steel, cement, refineries and chemicals — the five sectors most exposed to the EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism. The outlay follows the national CCUS roadmap released in December 2025 and signals that CCUS is no longer an afterthought but a strategic industrial policy priority.
Source: Carbon Credits / Down to Earth / Business Standard
🟢 Milestone
NTPC & IIT Bombay Complete India's First CO₂ Storage Test Well in Jharkhand
NTPC Limited, in collaboration with IIT Bombay, has completed India's first-ever CO₂ geological storage test well in Jharkhand, with a second monitoring well currently underway. The milestone is foundational — while modest in scale, it validates subsurface storage potential and lays the infrastructure groundwork needed before India can pursue commercial-scale CCS deployment across its industrial heartlands.
Source: OilPrice.com / ORF America

EU Environmental Crime Directive Enters Force 21 May 2026 — Criminal Penalties for Green Violations
EU member states must transpose the Environmental Crime Directive into national law by 21 May 2026, at which point it becomes enforceable across the bloc. The directive introduces criminal sanctions for serious environmental violations — including illegal waste shipments, significant water and air pollution, and species trafficking — marking a dramatic escalation from civil fines to criminal prosecution for corporate environmental misconduct in Europe.
Source: Hogan Lovells / ESG Compliance Outlook 2026
👀 Watch
China's Listed Companies Race to Meet 30 April 2026 Mandatory Sustainability Report Deadline
Companies listed on China's Shanghai, Shenzhen and Beijing stock exchanges are finalising their first-ever mandatory sustainability reports ahead of the April 30 deadline, following the CSRC's landmark sustainability disclosure guidelines. The shift from voluntary ESG storytelling to audit-grade, enforcement-ready reporting represents one of the most significant ESG regulatory transitions in Asia-Pacific — affecting thousands of listed entities across the world's second-largest economy.
Source: QIMA Blog / China Securities Regulatory Commission

🟢 Milestone
Northern Lights (Norway) CCS Hub Scales to 800,000 Tonnes Annual Storage Capacity in 2026
The world's first open-source CO₂ transport and storage infrastructure, operated by Northern Lights on the Norwegian continental shelf, is on track in 2026 to capture, liquefy and permanently sequester up to 800,000 tonnes of CO₂ annually from industrial customers across Europe. Over 15 years, the single project will store up to 12 million tonnes — the equivalent of removing 174,000 cars from the road every year — and is proving the commercial viability of cross-border CCS infrastructure for hard-to-abate sectors.
Source: Carbon Credits / Carbon Capture Europe Summit 2026

Cool Planet Technologies' Third-Generation Carbon Capture Membrane Passes Key Breakthrough Test
Cool Planet Technologies has announced the successful demonstration of its third-generation carbon capture membrane module, marking a transformative industrial milestone as the technology transitions from pilot stage to commercial-scale deployment. The membrane approach offers a potentially lower-cost and simpler alternative to conventional amine-based capture systems, and could be particularly attractive for mid-sized industrial emitters looking to decarbonise without massive capital expenditure.
Source: World Cement / Cool Planet Technologies
