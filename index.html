Where policy sprints and sustainability ambitions collide.
📋 Regulatory & Policy The rulebook just got a lot thicker.
🔥 Breaking
SEBI's BRSR Value Chain Disclosures Now Mandatory for Top 250 Listed Companies
SEBI's Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting framework enters a new phase in FY2025–26: the top 250 listed entities must now report ESG data across their value chains, with third-party assurance expected from 2026–27. Companies must cover suppliers and customers accounting for at least 2% of procurement or sales, capped at 75% of total value.
Source: Mongabay India / SEBI
👀 Watch
Parliament Recommends Dedicated ESG Oversight Body and Director-Level ESG Fiduciary Duties
India's Parliamentary Standing Committee on Finance has recommended creating a dedicated ESG oversight body within the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, and amending the Companies Act to make ESG objectives part of directors' fiduciary duties — effectively elevating sustainability to a boardroom legal obligation, akin to financial stewardship.
Source: Ropes & Gray / Parliament of India

Lupin Tops Global Pharma ESG Rankings with a Score of 91/100 in S&P CSA 2025
Lupin Limited secured a score of 91 out of 100 in the S&P Global Corporate Sustainability Assessment, placing it first among pharmaceutical companies worldwide and first across all sectors in India. The Mumbai-based drugmaker has now been included in the Sustainability Yearbook 2026 for the third consecutive year — a rare distinction in any industry globally.
Source: Business Standard / S&P Global
✅ Green Signal
IIFL Finance Scores 46 in Maiden S&P Global CSA — a 15-Point Leap in ESG Performance
IIFL Finance debuted in the S&P Global Corporate Sustainability Assessment with a score of 46, a sharp improvement from an independently derived baseline of 31. The NBFC describes the result as a shift from disclosure-led compliance to institutionally embedded ESG performance, signalling a maturing sustainability culture spreading deeper into India's financial sector.
Source: Business Standard / IIFL Finance
♻️ Carbon Capture & Decarbonisation Lab coats working overtime.
🔥 Breaking
India's Budget 2026-27 Commits $2.2 Billion to CCUS in Heavy Industry — A Historic First
In a landmark budgetary first, India's Union Budget 2026-27 ring-fenced ₹20,000 crore ($2.2 billion) over five years for carbon capture, utilisation, and storage (CCUS) technology. The dedicated scheme targets India's five hardest-to-abate sectors — power, steel, cement, refineries, and chemicals — which cannot be decarbonised by electrification alone.
Source: Carbon Credits / Government of India
🔬 Breakthrough
NTPC & IIT Bombay Complete India's First CO₂ Geological Storage Test Well in Jharkhand
NTPC Limited and IIT Bombay have drilled India's first CO₂ geological storage test well in Jharkhand, with a second monitoring well underway. While modest in scale, the milestone establishes the scientific and geological groundwork essential to any credible domestic CCUS infrastructure — a prerequisite for India achieving Net Zero by 2070.
Source: OilPrice.com / NTPC

📋 Regulatory & Policy Governments doing government things — but greener.
🔥 Breaking
40 Jurisdictions Now Aligned to ISSB Standards — Australia, Singapore & Hong Kong File First Reports in 2026
A total of 40 countries are now using or incorporating the ISSB's global sustainability disclosure standards, with Australia, Hong Kong, Malaysia, and Singapore issuing their first ISSB-aligned reports this year. The development marks a major step toward a unified global baseline for corporate climate disclosure, substantially narrowing the fragmentation that has plagued ESG reporting for a decade.
Source: QIMA / IFRS Foundation
👀 Watch
EU Packaging Regulation Becomes Enforceable in August 2026 — Supply Chains Scramble to Comply
The EU's Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation (PPWR) enters enforcement from August 2026, activating harmonised requirements for packaging design, labelling, recyclability, and extended producer responsibility. Companies reliant on EU markets and supply chains face a hard deadline with little remaining runway to align operations — adding to an already crowded ESG compliance calendar.
Source: Hogan Lovells
📡 In Numbers
UK SDR Goes Live — Scope 1 & 2 Emissions Disclosure Now Mandatory for In-Scope Entities
The UK's Sustainability Disclosure Requirements (SDR) came into effect on 1 January 2026, mandating Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions reporting based on 2025 data. The regime represents Britain's most significant climate reporting milestone since leaving the EU and positions the UK as a credible hub for ESG-aligned capital markets independent of Brussels.
Source: Institute of Sustainability Studies / UK Government

ISSB to Develop Global Nature-Related Reporting Standard — Exposure Draft Expected October 2026
The International Sustainability Standards Board has confirmed development of a global nature-related disclosure standard drawing on TNFD recommendations, with an Exposure Draft planned for October 2026. The move signals that biodiversity and ecosystem risk will soon be as regulated as carbon disclosures, fundamentally reshaping ESG materiality assessments for companies worldwide.
Source: Latham & Watkins / ISSB
👀 Watch
Global Corporations Race to Submit 2025 Sustainability Reports Ahead of April 30 Deadline
Major corporations worldwide are finalising their 2025 sustainability reports under pressure from tightened CSRD frameworks and investor demands, with a 30 April 2026 filing deadline for many European entities. Analysts note 2026 marks the shift from aspirational pledges to auditable, penalty-backed disclosure — a genuine inflection point for corporate ESG accountability.
Source: Harvard Law School Forum on Corporate Governance
♻️ Carbon Capture & Decarbonisation Sucking carbon from the air like it owes us money.
🔥 Breaking
World's Largest Direct Air Capture Plant Now Removing Up to 1 Million Tons of CO₂ Per Year
Carbon Engineering's Stratos facility in Texas is operational at commercial scale, capturing between 500,000 and 1 million metric tons of CO₂ annually — making it the world's largest direct air capture plant. Offtake agreements with Frontier, Amazon, and Airbus validate corporate demand for permanent carbon removal, as capture costs of $250–$600 per ton continue declining.
Source: Science Times / Carbon Engineering
🔬 Breakthrough
Cool Planet Technologies Unlocks Next-Gen Membrane Carbon Capture — Could Slash CCS Costs for Mid-Sized Emitters
Cool Planet Technologies has successfully tested its third-generation carbon capture membrane module, which avoids the costly chemical solvents used in conventional CCS. The lower energy footprint and simplified process could make carbon capture commercially viable for mid-sized industrial emitters — a segment largely excluded from current CCS economics.
Source: World Cement / Cool Planet Technologies
✅ Green Signal
Northern Lights CCS Now Storing 800,000 Tons of CO₂ Per Year — A Blueprint for European Infrastructure
Norway's Northern Lights project — a joint venture of Equinor, Shell, and TotalEnergies — is now capturing, liquefying, and permanently storing up to 800,000 tons of CO₂ annually, equivalent to taking 174,000 cars off the road each year. Designed to scale to 12 million tons over 15 years, it is widely regarded as the template for future European cross-border CCS networks.
Source: World Economic Forum / Northern Lights JV
