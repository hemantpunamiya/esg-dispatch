NABARD Launches National Climate Stack Innovation Challenge with Gates Foundation & Dalberg
NABARD has launched the National Climate Stack Innovation Challenge in partnership with the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation and Dalberg Advisors, offering ₹15 lakh in prizes for scientists and innovators developing high-accuracy climate forecasting solutions for rural India. The initiative targets hazard prediction models that integrate with existing infrastructure to democratize climate intelligence for small-scale farmers — shifting India from reactive risk management to forward-looking digital climate infrastructure.

India & UK Strike Deal to Align Unified Safety Standards for Green Hydrogen Value Chain
India and the United Kingdom have committed to establishing unified safety standards for green hydrogen across its entire value chain — production, storage, and transport — with Indian regulatory bodies including the Bureau of Indian Standards and PESO working alongside UK partners to align protocols with international best practices. The bilateral alignment, which incorporates AI-enabled monitoring systems, is designed to accelerate global competitiveness in green hydrogen by creating the secure infrastructure foundations essential for large-scale deployment.

India Hosts Pivotal SAF Policy Conclave 2026 During India Climate Week at Bharat Mandapam
India's SAF Association will convene the India SAF Policy Conclave 2026 on March 17 at Bharat Mandapam during India Climate Week, assembling policymakers, regulators, airlines, fuel producers, and investors to accelerate sustainable aviation fuel development. Key agenda items include India's feedstock potential, CORSIA-aligned certification pathways, financing mechanisms, and the framework for an upcoming SAF mandate — with a closed-door roundtable expected to produce actionable policy recommendations.

🟢 Policy
MNRE Notifies Green Methanol & Ammonia Standards Under National Green Hydrogen Mission
India's Ministry of New and Renewable Energy has issued Green Ammonia and Green Methanol Standards establishing clear greenhouse gas emission thresholds — 0.38 kg CO₂e/kg for ammonia and 0.44 kg CO₂e/kg for methanol — for products derived from renewable hydrogen. The standards deliver long-awaited regulatory clarity for India's green hydrogen industry, facilitating decarbonisation in the fertilizers and shipping sectors and strengthening India's competitive position as a reliable exporter of sustainable fuels.
Source: Solar Quarter / MNRE (3 March 2026)

🔥 Breaking
Singapore MAS Issues Finalized Climate Risk Management Guidelines for Banks, Investors & Insurers
The Monetary Authority of Singapore has released finalized guidelines requiring banks, asset managers, and insurers to assess and manage climate-related transition and physical risks through enhanced governance, improved data capabilities, and deeper client engagement. Notably, the guidelines caution institutions against indiscriminate divestment from high-risk companies, instead mandating multi-year engagement strategies that support orderly transitions — a significant reframe of how financial institutions should approach climate risk in portfolios.
Source: ESG Today (9 March 2026)
🔥 Hot
EU Formally Adopts 2040 Climate Target Mandating 90% Emissions Reduction
The European Union has formally approved its landmark 2040 climate target, committing the bloc to cutting greenhouse gas emissions by 90% compared to 1990 levels — one of the most ambitious climate targets adopted by any major economy to date. The framework also establishes stricter rules governing the quality and methodology of carbon credits and carbon removal, targeting greenwashing risk in Europe's rapidly expanding voluntary carbon market.
Source: ESG Times (6 March 2026)

🟢 Deal
Boeing Signs 40,000-Tonne Biochar Carbon Removal Deal with Carbonfuture Across the Global South
Boeing has signed a multi-year agreement with carbon removal provider Carbonfuture to offtake a minimum of 40,000 tonnes of durable CO₂ removal credits sourced from four biochar projects across the Global South. The deal is designed to address Boeing's hard-to-abate Scope 3 emissions from business travel under its "avoid first, remove second" carbon strategy adopted in 2024, and signals a shift by the aerospace industry toward long-duration, nature-based removal credits over conventional offset certificates.
Source: ESG Today (9 March 2026)

HSBC's Entire Climate Tech Investment Team Moves to Sustainable Investment Firm Bridges
HSBC Asset Management's climate technology investment team has transitioned en masse to join Bridges Fund Management, a specialist sustainable and impact investment firm, in a move that consolidates climate-focused investment expertise within a dedicated sustainability-only institution. The transition reflects a broader industry pattern of climate-specialist talent migrating from universal banks into purpose-built sustainable finance vehicles, as clients demand more focused and credible ESG mandates.

🔥 Milestone
Bill Gates' TerraPower Wins First-Ever US Construction Permit for Commercial Advanced Nuclear Reactor
TerraPower has received the first-ever construction permit from the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission for a commercial-scale advanced nuclear reactor, using its Natrium technology at the Kemmerer Unit One facility in Wyoming. The 345 MW baseload plant — with up to 500 MW capacity through integrated molten salt energy storage — is targeted to be operational by 2030, representing the most significant milestone for advanced nuclear energy development in the United States in a generation.
Source: ESG Today (9 March 2026)
🟢 Innovation
Shellworks Raises $15M to Scale Vivomer — a Home-Compostable, Microplastic-Free Packaging Material
UK biotech startup Shellworks has closed a $15 million Series A round led by French venture firm Alter Equity to scale its Vivomer material — a home-compostable, microplastic-free polymer made by fermenting waste biomass with microbes that mimics plastic's rigidity and flexibility. Already used by brands at Tesco and Whole Foods, co-founder Insiya Jafferjee stated that Vivomer is "already cost-competitive" with aluminum, glass, and paper alternatives at a fraction of plastic's production scale — addressing the persistent cost barrier that has stalled sustainable packaging at mass-market scale.
Source: ESG Today (9 March 2026)

LSEG Launches Sustainability Ratings Suite Covering 16,000+ Companies Across 220 ESG Indicators
The London Stock Exchange Group has unveiled LSEG Sustainability Ratings and Data, a new scoring suite covering more than 16,000 companies through a rules-based methodology with 220 standardised indicators across 12 themes, rating companies on a 0–5 scale aligned with ISSB, GRI, SASB, and ESRS frameworks. Designed for financial institutions to embed ESG data into automated workflows and regulatory reporting pipelines, the product directly addresses growing demand for explainable, AI-ready sustainability analytics that support capital allocation and transition-aligned investment decisions.

ESG Reporting Software Market Hits Record Trajectory — Projected to Reach $3.1B by 2033
The global ESG reporting software market is on track to reach $3.1 billion by 2033 at a 16.8% CAGR, driven primarily by mandatory regulatory frameworks including the EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive and ISSB standards forcing companies to automate emissions tracking and compliance reporting. North America currently leads with 38% market share while cloud-based deployment dominates at 61%, with banking and manufacturing sectors experiencing the steepest demand growth as audit-grade ESG data requirements become non-negotiable for institutional investors.
